About Downtown Lima

Downtown Lima, also known as the Historic Centre of Lima, is a UNESCO World Heritage site that boasts a rich history and stunning colonial architecture. Founded by Spanish conquistador Francisco Pizarro in 1535, Lima served as the capital of the Viceroyalty of Peru and was a major hub for trade and culture in South America. Visitors to Downtown Lima can explore iconic landmarks such as the Plaza Mayor, the Government Palace, and the Cathedral of Lima. The area is also home to numerous museums, churches, and historic buildings, each telling a unique story about Lima's past. In addition to its historical significance, Downtown Lima is a vibrant area filled with bustling markets, traditional restaurants, and lively street performances. Whether you're interested in history, culture, or simply soaking up the atmosphere, Downtown Lima offers something for every traveler.
